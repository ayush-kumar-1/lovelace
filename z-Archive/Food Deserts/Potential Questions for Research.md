Current research on food deserts shows that it is largely a problem of demand. Inadequate demand leads to a dearth of healthy food choices in food deserts. Economic incentives are one way of changing of quantity demanded of an item. Subsidies and taxes for food should be evaluated to determine how much of the demand problem is a pricing issue vs. how much is a time preference issue. 

Despite the current status of SNAP research, I expect some of the demand gap to result from price issues, but much of it should come down to underlying time preference.
